An ongoing process dynamically develops in such a manner that an individual can draw from a concept and the conclusion forms the basis on which another person arrives at a completely new concept. It is beneficial to note that research has numerous categories. Mass communication involves the study of how individuals, along with entities, relay information across mass media to big segments of the population concurrently. In this sense, communication research signifies the attempts to discover trends, or facts in the field of mass communication and mass media. The inconsistencies in research are qualitative, statistical, empirical, hypothetical, communicative, and logical (Flick, 374). Each group of research captivates its discrete limitations and downsides that prompt different scholars to establish different proofs by employing diverse systems of research. The ongoing nature of all studies is evolution of previous research inspirations (Zeffiro 179).
