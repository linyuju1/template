Introduction

The propagation of mass media is widely a global phenomenon. In particular, it is a commonality in developing nations, and with it comes exposure. It is, therefore, imperative to study the effects of media exposure, both the negatives and the positives. Many would argue that for the average Joe, most information comes from the media. The media has the propensity to reach millions of individuals, to influence, relay messages, or even educate. In retrospect, its effect on audiences is a major concern many. Awareness of the extent to which media exposure affects the populace is a requisite. In this sense, the study of mass media takes into account further progression in a number of areas. For instance, health communication utilizes mass media to reach to reach millions of individuals when conveying significant health campaigns. This is but one societal instance where mass media can be of benefit.
